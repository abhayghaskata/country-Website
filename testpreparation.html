Speaking

  1. The examination of this section is not on the same day asthe combined examination of Listening, Reading, and Writing.
  2. This is a personal one-to-one interaction with an examiner of about 11-14 minutes.
  3. The examination is divided into 3 parts:
  1. You will be asked a series of basic questions about your personal life such as yourself, your family, your hobbies, etc.
  2. You are given a CLUE CARD on which 1 subject is mentionedand you are asked to speak on that particular subject for about 2 minutesafter preparing for about 1 minute.
  3. You are asked some follow-up questions regarding your clue card and you are supposed to answer them.
  1. Confidence about speaking for yourself.
  2. Basic knowledge of sentence construction.
  3. Ability to listen to the examiner carefully before startingyour answer.
  1. PRONUNCIATION:
  2. your MOTHER TONGUE INFLUENCE should not be seen in your speech during the examination.
  1. FLUENCY& COHERENCE:
  2. Your speech should be fluent and without hesitations& your ideas are related to the questions asked by the examiner.
  1. GRAMMATICAL RANGE AND ACCURACY:
  2. your sentence construction in your speech is grammatically accurate and you usea wide range of complex and compound structures.
  1. LEXICAL RESOURCES:
  2. you use a wide range of vocabulary in your speech.

  1. SUMMARIZE SPOKEN TEXT :
  2. In this section, you have to listen to a prompt of 60-90 seconds and after the machine has stopped recording, you have to write a summary of the prompt you just listened to in about 50-70 words. 10 minutes are given to write your response.
  1. MULTIPLE CHOICE MULTIPLE ANSWERS:
  2. You have to listen to the prompt for about 30 – 90 seconds. You have to choose the correct summary from the given options according to the conversation played.

  2. After listening to the conversation you have to select the suitable option according to the conversation from the given options.

PTE Reading

  1. This section is 30 minutes long and contains 5 different question types which are described as follows:
  1. FILL IN THE BLANKS:
  2. A paragraph is given with several gaps in between them. To fill that gap you are given a drop-down menu consisting of several options and you have to select the appropriate option among them according to the context of the given text.
  1. MULTIPLE CHOICE MULTIPLE ANSWERS:
  2. A paragraph is given to you and several options are given, corresponding to the passage, you have to select all the correct options from the given options.
  1. REORDER PARAGRAPHS:
  2. You are given several parts of one passage in random order. You have to arrange them in the correct order according to the context of a passage.
  1. FILL IN THE BLANKS:
  2. This section is quite similar to the previous section; just the change is instead of a drop-down menu in each gap, a collection of words is given at the bottom of the passage and you have to select the appropriate word from those words and fill the given gap.
  1. MULTIPLE CHOICE SINGLE ANSWER :
  2. After reading the passage given, you have to choose one correct option among the varied given options.

PTE Speaking & Writing

  1. Pearson Test of English (PTE) is a COMPUTER-based examination widely accepted by foreign universities and colleges for abroad study and immigration.
  2. It consists of 4 independent modules namely Listening, Reading, Speaking, and Writing.
  3. Here the independent tasks are designed in such a way as to evaluate multiple skills in a particular task.
  4. The examination structure is as follows:
  5. Part 1: SPEAKING & WRITING
  6. It is 54- 67 minutes in duration.
  7. It contains 7 different question types.
  8. It is the longest section of the test
  9. 7 different types of questions are as follows:
  10. PERSONAL INTRODUCTION:
  11. This is an opportunity given to you to create your first impression. You have to give your basic details in this section. This section is not scored but it is sent to institutions where you wish to study along with your report. You are given 25 seconds to prepare and further 30 seconds to record your answer.
  12. READ ALOUD:
  13. You have to read the text which appears on the screen. Text is up to 60 words. Skills assessed in this part are speaking and reading.
  14. REPEAT SENTENCE:
  15. After listening to a sentence you have to speak the same sentence. The recording ofsentences by machine is between 3 to 9 seconds. You have to record your response in 15 seconds. Skills assessed in this part are listening and speaking.
  16. DESCRIBE IMAGE:
  17. An image appears on screen and you have to describe the given image in your own words in about 40 seconds. The skill assessed in this part is speaking.
  18. RETELL LECTURE:
  19. After listening to a lecture of about 90 seconds you have to paraphrase the lecture in 40 seconds. Skills assessed in this module are Listening and speaking.
  20. ANSWER SHORT QUESTION:
  21. After listening to a question, answer the same with a single word or group of words in 10 seconds. Skills assessed in this part are listening and speaking.
  22. SUMMARIZE WRITTEN TEXT
  23. After reading a passage you have to write a summary of that passage in one sentence. Usage of complex structures is required. Text is up to 300 words and 10 minutes are given to write your answer. Skills assessed in this part are speaking and writing.
  24. ESSAY WRITING:
  25. You have to write an essay on a given subject in between 200 to 300 words in 20 minutes.

READING SECTION:

  1. It includes 3 to 4 reading comprehension passages followed by a set of 10 questions per passage. Each passage is about 700 words long. The types of questions asked in the section are as follows:
  1. Factual and non-factual information
  2. Inference and rhetorical
  3. Reading vocabulary
  4. Sentence simplification
  5. Insert text
  6. Prose summary

LISTENING SECTION:

  1. The candidate will come across recorded English lectures and conversations.
  1. Listening lectures : 3 -4
  2. Each lecture duration : 3-5 min
  3. Number of questions per lecture: 6
  4. Listening conversation: 2-3
  5. Each conversation length: 3 min
  6. Number of questions per lecture: 5

SPEAKING SECTION:

  1. This section is subdivided into two subtasks.
  1. TASK 1 : Independent Task
  2. In this section, a candidate is required to speak and express one’s personal opinion on a familiar topic.
  1. TASK 2 : Integrated speaking task
  2. Questions 2 – 4 are related to campus situations and course topics. You are given 15 – 30 seconds to prepare for your answer and you have to speak for 45-60 seconds for each question.

WRITING SECTION:

  1. This section is also divided into two subtasks.
  1. TASK 1 : INTEGRATED WRITING TASK.
  2. In this section, a candidate has to listen to a short recording and read a short passage. After that one has to write the response based on information presented in the passage and the recording in 20 minutes.
  1. TASK 2 : INDEPENDENT WRITING TASK.
  2. In this task, a candidate is asked to write an essay on a given subject based on their knowledge and experience in 30 minutes.
